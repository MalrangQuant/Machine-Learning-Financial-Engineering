Probability structure¶

  • $X_{i}$ : The time spent by $i$-th shopper
  • $Z_{i} = \begin{cases} 1&\text{for male}\\ 0&\text{for female}\\ \end{cases}$
  • Probability densities

    $f_{1}(x)$: pdf of times for male shopper, i.e. $f_{1}(x) = f(x|z = 1)$
    $f_{0}(x)$: pdf of times for female shopper, i.e. $f_{0}(x) = f(x|z = 0)$
    $Z_{i} \sim Bernoulli(p)$
  • Joint density of $(X, Z)$:

    $f(x, z=1) = f(x|z = 1)p(z = 1) = pf_{1}(x)$
    $f(x, z=0) = f(x|z = 0)p(z = 0) = (1-p)f_{0}(x)$

    $\Rightarrow f(x, z) = \left[pf_{1}(x)\right]^{z}\left[(1-p)f_{0}(x)\right]^{1-z}$
  • Marginal density of X:

    $ \begin{align} f(x) = \sum_{z = 0}^{1} f(x, z) = f(x, z = 0) + f(x, z = 1) = pf_{1}(x) + (1-p)f_{0}(x) \end{align} $
  • Conditional density of Z given X:

    $ \begin{align} f(z|x) & = \frac{f(x, z)}{f(x)} \\ & = \frac{\left[pf_{1}(x)\right]^{z}\left[(1-p)f_{0}(x)\right]^{1-z}}{pf_{1}(x) + (1-p)f_{0}(x)} \\ & = \frac{\left[pf_{1}(x)\right]^{z}\left[(1-p)f_{0}(x)\right]^{1-z}}{\left[pf_{1}(x) + (1-p)f_{0}(x)\right]^{z}\left[pf_{1}(x) + (1-p)f_{0}(x)\right]^{1-z}} \\ & = \left(\frac{pf_{1}(x)}{pf_{1}(x) + (1-p)f_{0}(x)}\right)^{z}\left(\frac{(1-p)f_{0}(x)}{pf_{1}(x) + (1-p)f_{0}(x)}\right)^{1-z} \end{align} $
  • In generall, $k$-component mixture density:

    $ \begin{align} f(x; \theta) = \sum_{j = 1}^{k}p_{j}f(x; \theta_{j}), where \sum_{j = 1}^{k}p_{j} = 1, p_{j} \ge 0, for j = 1, \ldots, k \end{align} $
  • Example: Two-component normal mixture density

    $ \begin{align} f(x; p_{1}, p_{2}, \mu_{1}, \mu_{2}, \sigma_{1}, \sigma_{2}) = \frac{p_{1}}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_{1}^2}}exp\left(-\frac{\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)^2}{2\sigma_{1}^2}\right) + \frac{p_{2}}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_{2}^2}}exp\left(-\frac{\left(x-\mu_{2}\right)^2}{2\sigma_{2}^2}\right) \\ \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} E(X) = \mu^{*} = \sum_{j = 1}^{2}p_{j}\mu_{j} \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} Var(X) = \sum_{j = 1}^{2}p_{j}\sigma_{j}^2 + \sum_{j = 1}^{2}p_{j}\left(\mu_{j} - \mu^{*}\right)^2 \end{align} $

EM(Expectation-Maximization) Algorithm¶

  • (Maximum liklihood estimation, MLE) . (likelihood function) . ( n , $\theta$ $p$ .)

$ \begin{align} l(\theta) = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left[Z_{i}\cdot\left(log p + log f_{1}(x_i)\right) + (1-Z_{i})\cdot\left( log(1-p) + log f_{0}(x_{i})\right)\right] \end{align} $

  • $Z_{i}$ . E-step . , K-means .
  • E-step:
    $ \begin{align} \hat{Z_{i}} = E(Z_{i}|X_{i},\theta^{(t)}) = P(Z_{i} = 1|X_{i},\theta^{(t)}) = \frac{p^{(t)}f_{1}(x_{i}; \theta_{1}^{(t)})}{p^{(t)}f_{1}(x; \theta_{1}^{(t)}) + (1-p^{(t)})f_{0}(x_{i}; \theta_{0}^{(t)})} \end{align} $
  • E-step Z . E-step .

$ \begin{align} Q\left(\theta|\theta^{(t)}\right) = E\left(l(\theta)|\theta^{(t)}\right) = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left[\hat{Z_{i}}\cdot\left(log p^{(t)} + log f_{1}(x_i; \theta_{1}^{(t)})\right) + (1-\hat{Z_{i}})\left( log(1-p^{(t)}) + log f_{0}(x_{i}; \theta_{0}^{(t)})\right)\right] \end{align} $

  • . , .
  • M-step:

    $ \begin{align} \frac{\partial Q}{\partial p} = \frac{1}{p}\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{Z_{i}} - \frac{1}{1-p}\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left(1 - \hat{Z_{i}}\right) = 0 \\ \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} \Rightarrow p^{(t+1)} = \frac{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{Z_{i}}}{n} \end{align} $
  • E-step M-step . $p$ M-step .
  • Example: Two-component normal mixture density (revisited.)

    $ \begin{align} Q[\theta|\theta^{(t)}] = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{z}_{i}\cdot(\log p^{(t)}+\log \phi(x_i; \mu_{1}^{(t)}, \sigma_{1}^{(t)})) + \sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(1 - \hat{z}_{i}\right)\cdot(\log(1 - p^{(t)})+\log \phi(x_i; \mu_{2}^{(t)}, \sigma_{2}^{(t)})) \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} p^{(t+1)} = \frac{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{Z_{i}}}{n} \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} \hat{\mu_1}^{(t+1)} = \frac{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{z_{i}}x_{i}}{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{z_{i}}} \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} \hat{\mu_2}^{(t+1)} = \frac{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left(1 - \hat{z}_{i}\right)x_{i}}{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left(1 - \hat{z}_{i}\right)} \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} \hat{\sigma_1}^{(t+1)} = \frac{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{z_{i}}(x_{i}-\mu_1^{(t)})^2}{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\hat{z_{i}}} \end{align} $
    $ \begin{align} \hat{\sigma_2}^{(t+1)} = \frac{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left(1 - \hat{z}_{i}\right)(x_{i}-\mu_2^{(t)})^2}{\sum_{i = 1}^{n}\left(1 - \hat{z}_{i}\right)} \end{align} $
